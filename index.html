Air Quality of the World

Air quality is a critical environmental and public health concern affecting populations worldwide. This project explores various aspects of air pollution, including its impact on mortality rates, changes in emissions over time, and the most affected countries. Through data visualization, we aim to provide insights into the global state of air quality and its far-reaching consequences.

Global Deaths from Air Pollution in 2017

It can be seen that Central and Western Africa, along with parts of South Asia, exhibit the highest death rates, indicated by darker shades on the map. These regions face significant challenges in air quality, likely due to a combination of industrial emissions, vehicle exhaust, and biomass burning.

Changes in Air Pollutant Emissions (1970-2022)

Notably, sulphur dioxide (SO2) emissions have decreased significantly since the 1990s, likely due to stricter regulations and cleaner technologies. In contrast, ammonia (NH3) emissions have continued to rise, possibly linked to agricultural intensification.

Top 10 Most Air Polluted Countries

Bangladesh and Pakistan stand out with the highest concentration levels, significantly exceeding those of other nations on the list. The presence of several South Asian countries, including India and Nepal, points to a regional air pollution crisis. Factors contributing to this situation may include rapid urbanization, industrial growth, and geographical conditions that trap pollutants.

Death Rates by Age(per 100,000)

Pre-existing conditions and sickness in the elderly are often worsened by poor air quality. While younger groups show lower death rates due to better overall health, air pollution can still impact their long-term well-being. This data emphasizes the urgent need for air quality improvements, particularly to protect vulnerable older populations.
